## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the internal escalation procedure for handling intellectual property concerns, takedown requests, and enforcement actions related to print requests processed through 3D3D.

---

## 2. Escalation Triggers

A file or order may be escalated for review when:

### 2.1 External Triggers

- Designer or rights holder submits a complaint
- Third-party report received (e.g., community member, partner)
- Legal notice or demand letter received
- Law enforcement inquiry

### 2.2 Internal Triggers

- Obvious indicators suggest prohibited content (file names, metadata, known piracy sources)
- User has a documented history of flagged requests
- Order volume or pattern suggests commercial infringement activity
- Staff member identifies potential concern during routine operations

---

## 3. Escalation Tiers

### Tier 1: Initial Review (Operations Staff)

**Reviewer Role:** Fulfillment Operations

**Actions:**
1. Document the flag reason and source
2. Review file name, metadata, and any attached information
3. Cross-reference against Designer Opt-Out database
4. Check user's history for prior flags or warnings
5. Consult Prohibited Printing Criteria (PRINTING_POLICY.md Section 4)

**Decision Authority:**
- Approve order (clear of concerns)
- Refuse order (obvious violation)
- Escalate to Tier 2 (uncertain or complex)

**Response Timeline:** Within 24 hours of flag

---

### Tier 2: Elevated Review (Operations Lead)

**Reviewer Role:** Operations Lead or Senior Staff

**Actions:**
1. Deeper investigation of file origin (if identifiable)
2. Review context of the request (commercial vs personal use)
3. Assess potential brand or legal risk
4. Consider precedent and similar past cases

**Decision Authority:**
- Approve with documentation
- Refuse with user notification
- Issue formal warning to user
- Escalate to Tier 3 (legal complexity or high risk)

**Response Timeline:** Within 48 hours of escalation

---

### Tier 3: Legal/Executive Review

**Reviewer Role:** Founder or Legal Counsel

**Actions:**
1. Full legal risk assessment
2. Coordination with external legal counsel if necessary
3. Review for potential law enforcement involvement
4. Decision on account suspension or ban
5. External communication with complainants

**Decision Authority:**
- Final determination on order refusal
- Account suspension or termination
- Law enforcement referral
- Response to legal demands

**Response Timeline:** Within 5 business days

---

## 4. Law Enforcement Cooperation

### 4.1 Threshold for Involvement

3D3D will cooperate with law enforcement when:

- A valid legal order (warrant, subpoena, court order) is presented
- There is an imminent threat to public safety
- Required by Canadian federal or provincial law

### 4.2 Information Disclosure

We may disclose:

- User account information (name, email, address)
- Order history and metadata
- File metadata (names, timestamps — NOT file content after deletion)
- Transaction records

We will NOT disclose:

- File content that has been deleted per retention policy
- User data to entities without proper legal authority
- Information beyond the scope of the legal request

### 4.3 User Notification

Where legally permitted, we will notify users of law enforcement requests for their data.

---

## 5. Takedown Response Procedure

### 5.1 Receipt of Takedown Request

1. Log the request with timestamp and source
2. Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
3. Assign to appropriate escalation tier

### 5.2 Verification

1. Verify identity of complainant
2. Confirm ownership claim (portfolio, timestamps, publication records)
3. Match against flagged files if possible

### 5.3 Action

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Valid ownership, current order | Refuse order, notify user |
| Valid ownership, future prevention | Add to Designer Opt-Out blocklist |
| Invalid or incomplete claim | Request additional documentation |
| Counter-notice from user | Escalate to Tier 3 |

### 5.4 Notification

- User notified of refusal (neutral language, no accusation)
- Complainant notified of action taken within 5 business days
- No personal user information shared with complainant

---

## 6. Documentation Requirements

All escalation actions must be logged with:

- Date and time
- Reviewer identifier (role, not personal name in public docs)
- File/order reference
- Decision and rationale
- Communications sent
- Resolution status

Records retained for 7 years (Canadian legal requirement).

---

## 7. Appeals Process

Users may appeal decisions by:

1. Initial appeal to support@3d3d.ca (Tier 1 review)
2. Escalation to legal@3d3d.ca if unresolved within 5 business days (Tier 3 review)
3. Final determination by Founder or Legal Counsel

Designers may appeal by contacting legal@3d3d.ca directly.

---

## 8. Repeat Offense Handling

| Pattern | Action |
|---------|--------|
| 1st flag | Order refused, no formal warning |
| 2nd flag (12-month period) | Order refused, formal warning issued |
| 3rd flag (12-month period) | Pre-review required for future orders |
| 4th+ flag | Account suspension or permanent ban |

Founder may override in exceptional circumstances.
