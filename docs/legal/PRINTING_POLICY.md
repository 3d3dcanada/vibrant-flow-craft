## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy governs the handling of print requests, intellectual property considerations, and enforcement procedures for 3D3D Canada Inc. ("3D3D" or "the Company").

### 1.1 Scope of Services

3D3D operates two legally distinct service categories:

**(a) STL Commerce (Digital Product Sales)**

The sale of digital 3D model files (STL format) through the 3D3D Store. These files are:
- Designed by 3D3D
- Licensed to 3D3D by third-party designers under commercial terms
- Offered for free under permissive licenses (e.g., CC BY, CC0, public domain)

When a customer purchases a file from the 3D3D STL Store, they receive commercial printing rights as specified in the product license.

**(b) Print-as-a-Service (Manufacturing Services)**

The manufacturing of physical objects from user-provided digital files. In this capacity, 3D3D:
- Provides access to printing equipment
- Supplies materials (filament, resin)
- Provides labor and quality assurance
- Does not claim ownership of user-provided files
- Does not license, resell, or redistribute user-provided files

This service is comparable to a commercial print shop or copying service.

### 1.2 Governed Parties

This policy applies to:
- All employees and contractors of 3D3D
- Automated systems that process print requests
- Third-party service providers acting on behalf of 3D3D

## 2. Definitions

**User-Provided File**  
A digital 3D model file (e.g., STL, 3MF) uploaded by a customer for manufacturing through the Print-as-a-Service offering. Ownership and licensing responsibility rests with the user.

**3D3D STL**  
A digital 3D model file sold through the 3D3D Store, for which 3D3D holds ownership, licensing rights, or permissive redistribution rights.

**Print-as-a-Service**  
The manufacturing service in which 3D3D produces physical objects from user-provided files. 3D3D acts as a manufacturer, not a licensor or distributor of the design.

**Designer Opt-Out**  
A formal request by a designer to have their work blocked from being printed through the Print-as-a-Service offering. This is a good-faith mechanism, not a guarantee of absolute prevention.

**Prohibited Print**  
A print request that the Company refuses to fulfill based on the criteria set forth in Section 4 of this policy.

**Good-Faith Review**  
A reasonable, proportionate effort to assess a concern or complaint without conducting exhaustive verification of every file processed.

## 3. Acceptable Printing Criteria

The following categories of print requests are acceptable:

1. **User-Provided Files with Rights Affirmation**  
   Files uploaded by users who have confirmed, through the required acknowledgement checkbox, that they have the legal right to have the file printed.

2. **Files Purchased from the 3D3D STL Store**  
   Files for which the customer has obtained commercial printing rights through a valid purchase.

3. **Free and Open-License Files**  
   Files released under permissive licenses (e.g., CC BY, CC0, MIT, public domain) that permit commercial printing.

4. **Personal, Hobby, Educational, and Business Use**  
   Lawful use cases where the user has appropriate rights, including:
   - Personal projects
   - Educational purposes
   - Prototyping
   - Commercial production (when rights permit)

---

## 4. Prohibited Printing Criteria

The following categories of print requests are prohibited:

1. **Files Without Rights**  
   Files for which the user does not have legal authority to print, including designs obtained without the creator's permission.

2. **Commercial-Only Licenses Without Permission**  
   Files licensed for personal use only, or non-commercial use only, when the intended use is commercial and no appropriate license has been obtained.

3. **Known Pirated Sources**  
   Files identified as originating from repositories, websites, or sources known to distribute pirated or unauthorized content, when such origin is reasonably identifiable.

4. **Illegal or Prohibited Items**  
   - Weapons, weapon components, or firearm accessories
   - Drug paraphernalia
   - Items prohibited under Canadian federal or provincial law
   - Items requiring regulatory approval that has not been obtained

5. **Safety Bypass Items**  
   Items designed to circumvent safety mechanisms, security systems, or access controls.

6. **Repeat Infringers**  
   Users who have been documented engaging in a pattern of submitting Prohibited Prints, as defined by Section 9.6 of this policy.

## 5. Designer Opt-Out Program

### 5.1 Request Submission

Designers may request that their work be excluded from the Print-as-a-Service offering by submitting a request to: designers@3d3d.ca

The request must include:
- Designer name and contact information
- Proof of ownership or authorship (e.g., portfolio links, timestamps, publication records)
- Identification of the designs to be blocked (links, file names, hashes if available)

### 5.2 Information Recorded

Upon receipt of a valid opt-out request, the following is recorded:
- Designer contact details
- Design identifiers provided
- Date of request
- Status of request processing

### 5.3 Enforcement

Opt-out enforcement is applied on a reasonable, good-faith basis. 3D3D does not guarantee absolute prevention of all potential infringements, as:
- File modifications may evade detection
- Not all files can be verified against all possible designs
- The system operates on flagging, not pre-inspection

### 5.4 Response SLAs

- **Acknowledgement:** Within 48 hours of receipt
- **Action:** Within 5 business days of acknowledgement
- **Notification of Completion:** Within 2 business days of action

### 5.5 Appeals and Updates

Designers may:
- Update their opt-out record at any time
- Request removal of their opt-out record
- Appeal a decision by contacting legal@3d3d.ca

## 6. Review & Flagging Process

### 6.1 No Automated License Verification

3D3D does not inspect, scan, or verify the licensing status of every user-provided file. This policy reflects operational practicality and privacy considerations.

### 6.2 Triggers for Review

Files or orders may be flagged for manual review when:
- A complaint is received from a designer or rights holder
- A report is submitted by a user or third party
- Obvious indicators suggest prohibited content (e.g., file names, keywords, metadata)
- The user has a history of flagged requests
- Order volume or pattern suggests commercial infringement activity

### 6.3 Staff Actions Checklist

When a file is flagged:
1. Document the flag reason and source
2. Review the file name, metadata, and any attached information
3. Cross-reference against the Designer Opt-Out database
4. Consult the Prohibited Printing Criteria (Section 4)
5. Determine disposition: Approve, Refuse, or Escalate
6. Document decision with timestamp and staff identifier
7. Notify user if order is refused (see Section 9)

### 6.4 Documentation Requirements

All review actions must be logged with:
- Date and time
- Staff identifier
- File/order reference
- Decision and rationale
- Any communications sent

## 7. File Retention & Deletion

### 7.1 Retention Period

User-provided files are retained for a maximum of 14 calendar days following order completion or cancellation. This period allows for:
- Reprints due to defects
- Quality assurance review
- Customer service resolution

### 7.2 Immediate Deletion

Users may request immediate deletion of their files at any time by:
- Contacting privacy@3d3d.ca
- Using the delete function in their order history (when available)

Requests are processed within 2 business days.

### 7.3 Metadata Retention

The following may be retained beyond the file deletion period:
- Order metadata (date, specifications, status)
- Transaction records (as required by Canadian tax law: 7 years)
- Consent records (CASL compliance: permanent)
- Audit logs for security purposes

### 7.4 Storage Location

User-provided files and associated data are stored in Canada (Central region) via Supabase infrastructure, except where explicitly disclosed otherwise.

## 8. Royalty & Donation Handling

### 8.1 STL Store Purchases

When a customer purchases a file from the 3D3D STL Store, they receive commercial printing rights as specified in the product license. Designers are compensated according to their agreement with 3D3D.

### 8.2 Suggested Royalty Contribution

For files in the community section, designers are asked (not required) to contribute a suggested royalty of CAD $0.25 per sale. This is a voluntary contribution to support the platform.

### 8.3 Unclaimed Royalties

If royalty payments are collected but remain unclaimed:
- Funds are held for up to 1 calendar year
- After 1 year, 50% is donated to a registered Canadian charity supporting maker education
- The remaining 50% is allocated to community workshops, events, or educational initiatives

### 8.4 Record-Keeping

All royalty and donation transactions are:
- Logged with date, amount, and recipient category
- Available for audit upon request by the designer
- Subject to annual reconciliation

## 9. Refusal & Takedown Procedure

### 9.1 When a Print is Refused

A print may be refused when it meets one or more of the Prohibited Printing Criteria in Section 4.

### 9.2 User Notification

The user is notified via email with:
- Order reference number
- Statement that the order cannot be fulfilled
- General category of refusal (e.g., "This order has been declined as it does not meet our printing criteria.")
- Contact information for inquiries

Notifications use neutral, non-accusatory language.

### 9.3 Designer Notification

If a refusal is based on a Designer Opt-Out or takedown request, the designer is notified within 5 business days with:
- Confirmation that their request was honored
- No personally identifiable information about the user

### 9.4 Evidence Capture

For each refusal, the following is documented:
- Timestamp
- Request/order ID
- Reason for refusal
- Staff identifier
- Communications sent

### 9.5 Escalation Path

Disputes or appeals are handled as follows:
1. Initial inquiry to support@3d3d.ca
2. Escalation to legal@3d3d.ca within 5 business days if unresolved
3. Final determination by Founder or designated authority

### 9.6 Repeat Offense Handling

Users who submit three or more Prohibited Prints within a 12-month period may:
- Receive a formal warning
- Be subject to order review before fulfillment
- Have their account suspended
- Be permanently banned, at the discretion of the Company

Each escalation step is documented.

## 10. Good-Faith & Limitation Statement

### 10.1 Good-Faith Conduct

3D3D acts promptly and in good faith when concerns about intellectual property or prohibited content are raised. The Company does not knowingly facilitate infringement.

### 10.2 Limitations

3D3D cannot guarantee the prevention of all misuse. The measures described in this policy are:
- Proportional to the risk
- Respectful of user privacy
- Consistent with industry practice for manufacturing services

### 10.3 Intent

This policy supports compliance with Canadian intellectual property law and consumer protection standards. It is not intended as a surveillance mechanism.
